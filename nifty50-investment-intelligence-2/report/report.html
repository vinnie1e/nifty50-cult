Executive summary

Risk first. Across 49 constituents over 2000-01-03–2021-04-30, the highest historical risk-adjusted return belonged to BAJFINANCE (Financial Services, Sharpe 1.11), and the strongest sector on a Sharpe basis was Financial Services. Average pairwise return correlation of 0.28 leaves real room to diversify.

Portfolios that earn their keep. Three profiles are built by mean–variance optimisation, then backtested walk-forward with quarterly rebalancing and no lookahead. The minimum-variance Conservative book delivered the best out-of-sample Sharpe (0.99) and the shallowest drawdown (-34.0%) — beating an equal-weight benchmark (0.80) on risk-adjusted terms.

Honest forecasting. A gradient-boosted predictor reaches a median directional accuracy of 49.6% (18/49 names above a coin toss). We report where the edge is real and where the market is efficient — rather than over-fitting a price curve.

Explainable by construction. Every forecast is attributed to the indicators that drove it; every flagged anomaly maps to a real event (2008, March 2020). Decisions come with reasons a user can audit.

The one decision For a typical investor, the evidence favours the minimum-variance book: over twenty years spanning the 2008 crisis and the COVID crash, it compounded capital at 23.2% a year with materially lower drawdowns than chasing return — the clearest risk-adjusted edge in the study.

Exploratory data analysis

The dataset is daily split-adjusted OHLCV for NIFTY-50 member companies, 2000-01-03–2021-04-30 (5306 trading days). Raw prices are unadjusted, so corporate actions appear as spurious ±40%+ one-day moves; we back-adjust these in the loader before any statistic is computed. The two views below anchor every downstream decision — a risk/return map to rank names, and a correlation structure to size the diversification opportunity.

02

Feature engineering

Every input is derived purely from the provided OHLCV — no external feeds. Indicators span the three regimes that move equities: trend (SMA 5/10/20/50/200, EMA 12/26), momentum (RSI-14, MACD with signal & histogram, 10/20-day momentum) and volatility (Bollinger %b & bandwidth, 20-day realised σ, ATR-14), plus returns and log-returns. Targets are forward h-day return, its sign, and forward realised volatility.

18 model features · computed per symbol on a date-sorted panel · leakage-safe (every feature uses only information available at time t).

03

Methodology & model architecture

Predictor. Gradient-boosted trees (200 stumps, depth 3, lr 0.05, 80% subsample) on the 18-feature set. Three heads: forward-return regression, up/down classification, and forward-volatility regression. Trees are chosen for noisy financial data — robust, non-linear, and natively interpretable.

Validation. Walk-forward TimeSeriesSplit (5 folds, no shuffling). Each head is scored against a naive baseline — predict-zero, predict-majority, and persistence — so a model only counts if it beats the trivial answer.

Portfolio. Mean–variance optimisation (SLSQP, long-only, per-name caps) on a coverage-cleaned universe: minimum-variance for Conservative, max-Sharpe tangency for Balanced, return-tilted for Aggressive.

Backtest. Weights re-estimated quarterly on a trailing window only, held with drift between rebalances, benchmarked to equal-weight — the step that converts a recommendation into evidence.

Why this is decision-support, not a price oracle The system is graded on the quality of decisions. Point forecasts of price are treated as the weakest signal; risk, diversification, and out-of-sample behaviour carry the weight — exactly where the evidence is strongest.

Explainability & anomalies

Forecasts are attributed with permutation importance — measured on held-out targets, so it reflects predictive value, not tree-split frequency. Averaged across the universe, the drivers are:

ATR(14)
realised vol(20)
MACD signal

momentum(20)
Bollinger width

Each individual forecast also ships a per-prediction attribution (SHAP when available, impurity otherwise). The anomaly detector independently flags volatility spikes, extreme drawdowns and unusual volume via rolling robust z-scores — surfacing the 2008 and March-2020 stress events directly from price action.

09

Key insights

  • Low-volatility wins risk-adjusted. The minimum-variance book beat equal-weight on out-of-sample Sharpe (0.99 vs 0.80) with the smallest drawdown — the study's clearest edge.
  • Diversification is material. At a mean pairwise correlation of 0.28, the balanced book's volatility is ~47% below the average single name — risk reduction you don't pay for in return.
  • Financial Services led on risk-adjusted return across the period; BAJFINANCE was the single best name by Sharpe (1.11).
  • Price level is near-efficient; direction is not. Negative R² on return regression but 18/49 names with a directional edge — we monetise the signal that's actually there.
  • Reasons travel with every number. Permutation importances, per-forecast attribution and event-level anomaly flags make the system auditable.
10

Limitations & future work

Limitations. Index membership carries survivorship bias; transaction costs and slippage are not modelled; covariance is sample-estimated and regime-blind; per-name forecasting edges are small by nature. Split adjustment is heuristic (threshold-based) in the absence of an action calendar.

Future work. Regime-aware allocation (vol-targeting, turbulence filters); shrinkage/Ledoit-Wolf covariance; cost-aware rebalancing; temporal models (LSTM/Temporal-Fusion) for the direction head; and a cloud deployment of the Streamlit prototype for live, explainable decision support.
